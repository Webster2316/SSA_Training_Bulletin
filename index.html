MCAOC/Advisory/2026/020 Patches Available for Actively Exploited Checkpoint Vulnerability

All Members

Documents:

1. Checkpoint advisory CVE-2026-50751.pdf
2. IP.txt

Action Required:

Members are requested to work with their vendors to:

i. assess whether affected Checkpoint products (Security Gateways and Spark Firewall) are in use within their organisation;
ii. apply the relevant patches or mitigations;
iii. review the provided IOC list, block the identified IOCs, and scan networks and systems accordingly.

Members shall remain cyber vigilant and report any suspicious or abnormal cyber activities to MCAOC immediately.

Background:

Checkpoint has released a patch for CVE-2026-50751, a critical vulnerability (CVSS 9.3) affecting Mobile Access / SSL VPN, Remote Access VPN, and Spark Firewall. The vulnerability has been exploited in the wild since 7 May 2026, with some attackers deploying ransomware.

Affected products:

MC(26)68 – Transits Through Omani Coastal Waters in the Strait of Hormuz

MC(26)68 – Transits Through Omani Coastal Waters in the Strait of Hormuz

International, Technical

The United States Navy (USN) has established a monitoring programme for vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz via the Omani coastal route. The USN does not provide convoy coordination, escorts, or vessel prioritisation, and assumes no responsibility for ships using this route. Operators should conduct thorough threat assessments in line with BMP Maritime Security and industry guidance on safe management of Strait of Hormuz transits. Queries may be directed to the US Fifth Fleet NCAGS (m-ba-navcent-ncags@us.navy.mil). Members are reminded to verify that any correspondence originates from a us.navy.mil domain.
