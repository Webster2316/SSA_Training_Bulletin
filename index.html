MC(26)66 – Proposed Transit Corridor for Fertilizer Cargoes in the Persian Gulf

MC(26)66 – Annex A – White Ships Corridor Draft

International, Technical

Action Required

Members are requested to review the proposed "White Ships Corridor" initiative for fertilizer cargoes transiting the Strait of Hormuz and provide comments or feedback to anis@ssa.org.sg, if any.

Background

The ICS has been invited to support Operation "White Ships Corridor", a UN-supported initiative to establish a time-bound humanitarian corridor allowing clearly identified vessels carrying fertilizer cargoes to transit safely through the Strait of Hormuz. This aims to restore fertilizer flows ahead of the 2026–27 planting seasons and support global food supply chains, complementing ongoing IMO efforts and coordinated through a dedicated UN task force.

MC(26)68

MC(26)68 – Transits Through Omani Coastal Waters in the Strait of Hormuz

MC(26)68 – Transits Through Omani Coastal Waters in the Strait of Hormuz

International, Technical

The United States Navy (USN) has established a monitoring programme for vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz via the Omani coastal route. The USN does not provide convoy coordination, escorts, or vessel prioritisation, and assumes no responsibility for ships using this route. Operators should conduct thorough threat assessments in line with BMP Maritime Security and industry guidance on safe management of Strait of Hormuz transits. Queries may be directed to the US Fifth Fleet NCAGS (m-ba-navcent-ncags@us.navy.mil). Members are reminded to verify that any correspondence originates from a us.navy.mil domain.

CAN(26)06

CAN(26)06 – Suez Canal Authority Announces Increase to Surcharges on Transit Dues Effective from 15 July 2026

SCA Navigation Circulars

International

Documents:

1.Periodical – amendment of circ. 3.2022 from 15.07.2026
2.Periodical – amendment of circ. 3.2022 from 15.07.2026 (SP)
3.Periodical – amendment of circ. 8.2022 from 15.07.2026
4.Periodical – amendment of circ. 12.2022 from 15.07.2026
5.Periodical – amendment of circ. 10.2022 from 15.07.2026

On 9 June 2026, the Suez Canal Authority (SCA) announced a 12% increase to existing surcharges on transit dues for all vessels (excluding passenger ships), effective 15 July 2026. The surcharge increase varies by vessel type (e.g., laden crude oil/product tankers: 37%, ballast: 27%; containerships: 12%). The SCA circulars did not provide rationale or methodology.

ICS is seeking clarification from the SCA, noting the lack of prior consultation and insufficient advance notice. ICS is also drafting a formal letter, in coordination with other international associations, requesting a delay in implementation. Members with comments or questions may contact anis@ssa.org.sg.
MLC(26)14

MLC(26)14 – Entry into Force of the International Convention on Liability and Compensation for Damage in Connection with the Carriage of Hazardous and Noxious Substances by Sea (2010 HNS Convention)

Legal and Insurance

The IMO has confirmed that the 2010 HNS Convention will enter into force on 29 November 2027 after all required criteria were met by 12 Contracting States. Key implementation arrangements include establishing the HNS Fund in London alongside the IOPC Funds, with interim operations supported by the 1992 Fund.

Preparations are underway to handle significant administrative requirements, particularly the issuance of insurance certificates for vessels carrying hazardous substances. With about 65,000 vessels potentially affected, States are using the 18-month lead time to coordinate processes, develop guidance for shipowners, and assess their capacity to issue certificates efficiently.

Kindly direct any comments and questions to anis@ssa.org.sg.
